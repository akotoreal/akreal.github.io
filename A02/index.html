TOKYO

Japan's Vibrant Capital

About Tokyo

Tokyo, Japan's vibrant capital, is a bustling metropolis where tradition meets modernity. Originally a fishing village, it transformed into a major political center in the 17th century. Today, the city features iconic landmarks like Tokyo Tower and Skytree, historic sites such as Senso-ji Temple, and dynamic neighborhoods like Akihabara and Harajuku. With a rich culinary scene and a blend of old and new, Tokyo captivates millions of visitors annually.

Tokyo's catchphrase is "Tokyo Tokyo Old meets New," emphasizing the city's unique blend of rich tradition and cutting-edge modernity. The phrase highlights Tokyo's ability to honor its cultural heritage while embracing innovation and contemporary trends.

Tourist Spots

Tokyo Tower

A symbol of Tokyo, this Eiffel Tower-inspired structure offers panoramic views of the city, including Mount Fuji on clear days.

Tokyo Skytree

The tallest structure in Japan, providing stunning views and dining options in the sky.

Senso-ji Temple (Asakusa)

Tokyo’s oldest and most famous Buddhist temple. Visitors can also explore the Nakamise Shopping Street leading up to the temple.

Harajuku

Famous for its youth culture, quirky fashion stores, and colorful street art. It's the epicenter of Tokyo’s street fashion scene.

Shibuya Crossing

One of the busiest pedestrian crossings in the world. The surrounding area is famous for its nightlife, shopping, and entertainment.

Meiji Shrine (Shibuya)

Meiji Shrine in Shibuya is a tranquil Shinto shrine honoring Emperor Meiji and Empress Shōken, set within a vast forest. It offers a peaceful escape and traditional rituals in the heart of Tokyo.

Imperial Palace and Gardens

The residence of Japan's Imperial Family. While the inner grounds are closed to the public, visitors can explore the East Gardens.

Akihabara (Electric Town)

A paradise for tech lovers, anime, and manga enthusiasts. The area is full of electronic shops, gaming arcades, and themed cafés.

Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden

One of Tokyo’s largest parks, offering traditional Japanese, French, and English-style gardens. A peaceful escape in the heart of the city.

Odaiba

A futuristic entertainment island in Tokyo Bay, home to teamLab Borderless (digital art museum), shopping malls, amusement parks, and the giant Unicorn Gundam statue.

Roppongi Hills and Mori Art Museum

A sophisticated area known for its luxury shopping, dining, and contemporary art exhibits at the Mori Art Museum.

Tsukiji Outer Market

Although the inner wholesale fish market moved to Toyosu, the outer market still offers fresh seafood, sushi restaurants, and food stalls.

Yoyogi Park

A massive park next to Meiji Shrine, popular for picnics, jogging, and cultural festivals, especially beautiful during cherry blossom season.

Ueno Park and Zoo

A large public park with museums, shrines, a zoo, and seasonal cherry blossoms. The Tokyo National Museum and Ueno Zoo are major attractions here.

Ginza District

Tokyo’s upscale shopping and dining district, known for luxury stores, high-end restaurants, and the famous Kabuki-za Theater for traditional Kabuki performances.

Tokyo Disneyland & DisneySea

World-class theme parks offering family-friendly entertainment, with Tokyo DisneySea offering a unique ocean-themed experience.
